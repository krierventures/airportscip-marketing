01 · The problem

Airport grants are complex.
Managing them shouldn't be.

Most airports track millions in FAA funding across spreadsheets, email threads, and shared drives. One missed deadline or wrong NPR can cost a year of entitlement funding.

01 Data sprawl

Spreadsheets that break

Allocations, drawdown schedules, and phase budgets spread across a dozen files — all out of sync, one bad formula from a compliance problem.

02 Timing risk

Deadlines that sneak up

Pre-application windows, obligation dates, closeout reports — each stage has hard FAA deadlines. Miss one and you risk this year's and next year's entitlement.

03 Duplicate effort

Forms re-entered by hand

The SF-424, FAA 5100-100, and SF-270 all pull from the same project data — but it gets typed in from scratch every time, with no guarantee of consistency.

How it works

From CIP
to grant closeout

1

Build your CIP

Add projects and phases. Classify each one as Necessary or Related, assign FAA work codes, and let the platform calculate NPR scores automatically.

2

Link your grants

Create grants and allocate funding to specific phases. Track federal share, local match, and BIL/IIJA amounts separately for each project component.

3

Work the lifecycle

Move each grant through Pre-App → Application → Award → Active → Closeout with stage-specific checklists and document tracking at every step.

4

Generate and submit

Export pre-filled FAA forms. Track drawdowns. Document everything. Close grants cleanly — and start the next cycle without starting from scratch.
